Dec.09.2024

Our publication, "Human-Machine Interaction in Automated Vehicles: Reducing Voluntary Driver Intervention", has been selected and accepted by "Transportation Research Part C: Emerging Technologies". The journal has a high impact factor of 7.6. This research was conducted on driving simulators running Chrono::HIL middleware, a research-oriented project developed for my master's thesis. Chrono::HIL is an open-source middleware for distributed vehicle dynamics simulation, built upon C++/DDS/CUDA with sensor simulation and X-in-the-loop support. This publication showcased that the project I handrolled is comparable to commercial solutions such as NADS MiniSim or Real-Time Technologies in terms of producing academically credible data.
