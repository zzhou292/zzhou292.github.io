I am excited to share that my PhD journey has gained new sponsors: Intuitive Machines, Michelin, and NASA, as part of the Moon Racer project. Intuitive Machines, the prime contractor for the Lunar Terrain Vehicle Services Feasibility Assessment, will be the primary sponsor supporting the continuation of my PhD study. Dec.09.2024

Our publication, "Human-Machine Interaction in Automated Vehicles: Reducing Voluntary Driver Intervention", has been selected and accepted by "Transportation Research Part C: Emerging Technologies". The journal has a high impact factor of 7.6. This research was conducted on driving simulators running Chrono::HIL middleware, a research-oriented project developed for my master's thesis. Chrono::HIL is an open-source middleware for distributed vehicle dynamics simulation, built upon C++/DDS/CUDA with sensor simulation and X-in-the-loop support. This publication showcased that the project I handrolled is comparable to commercial solutions such as NADS MiniSim or Real-Time Technologies in terms of producing academically credible data.

May.23.2024

I passed my PhD Preliminary Exam on May 23rd, 2024. I would like to give special thanks to my PhD committee members - Prof. Dan Negrut, Prof. Josiah Hanna, Prof. Wei Wang, Prof. Xiangru Xu, and Prof. Jinlong Wu. My PhD work focuses on designing and architecting a scalable robotics simulator with distributed multi-physics simulation leveraging heterogeneous computing. I aim to make efforts to increase the predictive power of robotics simulators, and enable multi-robot imitation and reinforcement learning in complex environments, driven by physics-based simulations. Additionally, I anticipate that part of my PhD work will contribute to bridging the gap between simulation and reality in a multi-agent context.
