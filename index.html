About

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, double majoring in Computer Science and Mechanical Engineering.

I'm an experienced c++ developer, with a focus on robotics and autonomous vehicle control design, physics-based simulation, and game development. I enjoy problem-solving and coding. Always strive to bring 100% to the work I do.

My research focuses on the development and validation of digital twins and their applications in the design of autonomy stacks for robotic systems, autonomous vehicles, and smart infrastructure. My research experiences have allowed me to develop strong programming skills, knowledge in high-performance computing, deep understandings in multi-body dynamics and physics simulation, and computer graphics. I am a member of the Simulation-Based Engineering Lab (SBEL) at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, and an active developer of the open-source multi-physics simulation engine Project Chrono.

I have worked with technologies such as C++, C for Embedded Systems, Python, ROS, MySQL, Java, Embedded Software, Docker, SolidWorks, and Ansys.

I am passionate about developing complex applications that solve real-world problems impacting millions of users.

  • Libraries and Frameworks: NumPy, Pandas, OpenCV, scikit-learn, matplotlib, Vulkan, ROS/ROS2, Keras, TensorFlow, PyTorch
  • Tools & Technologies: Git, AWS, Docker
